# Project Self-Assessment

## Purpose

Use this after completing a project or exercise.

## Project

[Project name or description]

## Goal

[What was the intended outcome?]

## Time Constraint

[How much time was allocated?]

## 1. Outcome Review

- Did I complete the core goal?
- What works as expected?
- What is incomplete or broken?

## 2. Decision Review

- What were the key decisions I made?
- Were they simple or overly complex?
- What would I simplify if I did this again?
## 3. Planning vs Execution

- Did I spend enough time planning?
- Did I jump into code too early?
- Where did planning help or fail?
## 4. Scope Control

- Did I stay within the time constraint?
- Where did I overbuild?
- What should I have skipped?
## 5. Code Quality (High-Level)

- Is the structure clear?
- Would someone else understand it quickly?
- Are there obvious improvements?

## 6. Risk and Failure Awareness

- What broke or almost broke?
- What did I not anticipate?
- Where was the system fragile?
## 7. What I Learned

- What did I understand better after this?
- What confused me?
- What would I research next?
## 8. What I Would Do Differently

- What would I change if I restarted?
- What would I keep the same?
## 9. Signals of Over-Engineering

Check any that apply:

- Added unnecessary abstraction
- Built features not required for the goal
- Spent too long on low-impact details
- Optimized prematurely

## 10. Final Reflection

- Was my approach aligned with the goal?
- Did I prioritize clarity, speed, or correctness appropriately?
- What is one thing I will improve on the next project?
